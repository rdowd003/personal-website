How I lead

I'm not looking for followers. I'm looking for authors.

A working philosophy, written down and lived out loud. Three of fourteen cards — the rest are worth the read.

Who I am

I build the conditions, then steer the ship.

Part servant, part transformational, fully an architect. The sharpest structure is the one that runs without its architect.

"I set the goal and the guardrails, then let the team choose the how. If I'm in every decision, I've designed it wrong."
Why it matters

Arbitrary authority wastes people.

When a decision comes down by rank instead of reason, capable people learn their judgment doesn't count — so they stop bringing it.

"'Because I said so' is a last resort, not a first one. If I can't name the reason, the decision isn't ready."
My mission

Make authorship contagious.

Surface the patterns most people miss and put what I know in the open — not to be relied on, but to dissolve the reliance.

"When someone asks me for the answer, I show them how I'd find it, then hand it back. Next time they won't need to ask."
